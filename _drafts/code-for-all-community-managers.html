The Community Manager will work with the CfAll governing partners on six continents and . A Community Manager will support the creation and disbursement of adaptable tools and best practices that are sensitive to cultural, social and political realities across the world.NDI staff to become a permanent globally-focused staff member of the network. Given that the problems being addressed by the CfAll community―transparency, accountability, trust in government, citizen engagement―are widespread around the world, this staff member will help to deliver upon the potential to leverage the network to support affiliate organizations and networks in consolidating democracies that are aspiring to effectively use civic technology These could take the form of training modules, best practice guides, case studies, web conferences and online mentorships. The Community Manager will also assist in the creation of “expert groups,” informal groupings of skilled members of the CfAll community, that can function as support groups and mentors to affiliate partners, government partners and other members of the civic tech community looking to solve specific problems or learn new skills. The Community Manager will coordinate creation of reports, writings or media that provide lessons to others in the civic tech community. This storytelling component of the Manager’s responsibility may involve contracting with writers or journalists. The Community Manager will reach out to organizations around the world that believe in the same values inviting them to join Code for All forums and activities.

After over a decade spent in academia, directly observing and analyzing social phenomena in the field of human rights and humanitarian law, with a strong focus on challenged communities, Bogdan has co-founded Code4Romania in early 2016. The aim was to put technology at work for social change in Romania. In just a year, Code4Romania rapidly grew from an idea into a movement, encompassing a vibrant community of 500 volunteers of various specializations spread over 11 time zones working on over 20 projects.
